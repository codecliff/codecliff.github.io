PhotoGlimmer User Guide

PhotoGlimmer is a free and open-source image editor distributed under the LGPL license. It is designed to enhance lighting on people or pets in photos with complex or stunning backgrounds, ensuring that the subjects look just as good as their surroundings.

Key Features

  • Automatic detection and enhancement of people/pets using AI.
  • Manual selection and adjustment using rectangular regions.
  • Sliders to control exposure, brightness, saturation, blending, etc.
  • Supports toggling between foreground and background editing.
  • Clipboard and foreground export.
  • No GPU requirement
  • Curve based brightness tweaking (optional, experimental)

Getting Started

  1. Click the Browse button or press Ctrl+O to select an image.
    1. The image opens in the editor. Any people or pets detected will appear as a grayscale segmentation mask in the left panel.
    2. Use the Threshold slider to adjust the automatic detection if necessary.
    3. üí°üñå Adjust Brightness and Saturation sliders to enhance the selected area.
    4. Use Edge Blur to improve blending between the foreground and background.
    5. Toggle between editing Background and Foreground using the BG/FG switch at the top.
    6. Use BG Blur to blur the background for aesthetic effects.
    7. Adjust the Preserve slider to control the overall extent of changes applied.
  2. Click the Save button or press Ctrl+S to export your final image.
  3. To reset all changes at any time, click the ‚èÆ button.
  4. Hold down the right mouse button over the image to temporarily view the original photo.

Enhancements

  • Draw rectangular selections over people or body parts to apply targeted enhancements.
  • Rectangles guide the AI to focus analysis on specific areas of the image.
  • Open Tools > Preferences to configure application behavior.
  • üí°üñå You can switch from sliders to curves for advanced control (experimental).
  • Apply noise reduction with the Denoise button (may impact performance).
  • Use Tools > FG to clipboard to copy the selected foreground and paste it into another editor.

Working with Rectangles

  • Click and drag on the image to draw multiple rectangles for selective enhancement.
  • Click on a rectangle to move it around the image.
  • Resize a rectangle by dragging its edges (corners are not resizable).
  • To hide all rectangles, click on the image and press H. Press H again to show them.
  • To delete a rectangle, select it and press the Del key.
Note: When rectangles are hidden, you cannot draw new ones. Press H to unhide them.

Usage FAQ:

Q: What if the automatic detection is not accurate?

A: You can adjust the detection using the Threshold slider to grow or shrink the AI-generated mask. You can also draw rectangles to manually guide or override the detection.

Q: Can I manually choose which parts of the photo to enhance?

Q: What do the "BG Blur" and "Preserve" sliders do?
